Markov Random Fields: Quantifying Neuronal Spikes and Forest Fires

by Fabiana Ferracina and Jacob Pennington (WSU Vancouver)

Our goal is to apply Markov Random Fields to the problem of image segmentation for the purpose of classifying neuronal activity and detecting forest fires.

Researchers have shown that the MRF model can spatially quantify neuronal activity data:

Further exploration of model assumptions is necessary in order to apply it to in vivo or ex vivo imaging data.

In Ecology, MRFs can be used on remote sensing and satellite imagery in order to classify land types and detect forest fires.

With respect to fire, patches of land are much like neurons which with repect to firing can be active, refractory, or quiescent.
